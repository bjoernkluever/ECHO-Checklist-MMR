ECHO Proposal Quality‑Assurance Checklist - Myanmar HIP 2026

Use this checklist to quality‑assure proposals before submission to DG ECHO. It integrates rejection reasons shared and the Myanmar‑specific provisions of the 2026 HIP and Technical Annex.

A. Needs Assessment & Evidence Base

  • Is the needs assessment comprehensive, contextualized, recent, and disaggregated (quantitative and qualitative)?
  • Is the analysis specific to the proposed geographic area and population groups (not generic), and does it directly justify the action?
  • Are vulnerabilities and target groups evidenced with sources and dates, and aligned to the most acute needs?
  • Is humanitarian advocacy grounded in coordinated, evidence‑based analysis?
  • Where feasible/safe, is there consultation or coordination with relevant authorities; if not, is a clear justification provided?

B. Response Analysis & Strategy

  • Is there a coherent, detailed implementation strategy (methodology, sequencing, delivery mechanisms, targeting, monitoring)?
  • Is the response logic fully aligned with the logical framework (results, outputs, indicators, assumptions)?
  • Are operational/protection/access/contextual risks clearly analyzed with concrete mitigation measures?
  • If working through community/camp management committees or local structures, are risks (e.g., exclusion, elite capture) and mitigations explicit?
  • Are conflict‑sensitivity and Do No Harm considerations integrated and tracked?

C. Alignment With ECHO Policies & Myanmar HIP 2026

  • Does the intervention focus on life‑saving multi‑sector assistance for IDPs, conflict‑affected and disaster‑affected populations (incl. hard‑to‑reach areas)?
  • Are sector choices aligned with Myanmar HIP 2026 priorities: protection (incl. mine action/IHL dissemination/GBV/CAAC/PSS), food/basic needs, health and nutrition, shelter/NFIs, WASH, emergency multi‑sector assistance, and EiE (return to learning via temporary opportunities)?
  • Is localisation a core feature (fair partnerships, capacity strengthening, risk sharing, adequate share of overheads to local partners)?
  • Is there coordination and clear complementarity with FLER/emergency mechanisms, and cluster/Area of Responsibility reporting?
  • Are Minimum Environmental Requirements (MER) applied across sectors, including WASH and Shelter & Settlements designs?
  • Is the proposal free of standalone DP components in Myanmar (2026 targeted DP budget is not allocated to Myanmar) while mainstreaming preparedness where relevant?
  • Does the proposal operationalise the Nexus where appropriate without diluting the life‑saving focus?

D. Proposal Structure, Detail & Internal Coherence

  • Is the proposal well‑structured and complete (Single Form sections fully detailed, no vague descriptions)?
  • Are activities, logframe elements, and budget internally consistent across all sections?
  • Are indicators SMART, time‑bound, and aligned with DG ECHO Key Result Indicators (KRIs) where applicable (avoid custom if a KRI exists)?
  • Is the DG ECHO Protection Mainstreaming KOI (PMKOI) applied across sectors, and a Protection outcome indicator included for protection actions?
  • Are abbreviations, methodologies, and technical concepts clearly explained?

E. Results Framework Quality

  • Are intended results clearly defined, realistic, and derived from the needs assessment?
